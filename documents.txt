Aspen, Colorado is one of the most famous ski resorts in the United States. It offers over 5,000 acres of skiable terrain with 300+ trails. The ski season runs from November to April.
Vail, Colorado is the largest ski resort in the United States with over 5,200 acres of terrain. It features seven miles of back bowls and world-class amenities. Vail receives approximately 300 inches of annual snowfall.
Park City, Utah is home to the largest ski resort in the United States with 7,300 acres of terrain. It hosted events during the 2002 Winter Olympics and offers 300+ trails across two interconnected mountains.
Whistler Blackcomb in British Columbia, Canada is the largest ski resort in North America with over 8,100 acres of terrain. It was a host venue for the 2010 Winter Olympics and receives over 400 inches of annual snowfall.
Lake Tahoe, California offers multiple world-class ski resorts including Heavenly, Palisades Tahoe, and Northstar. The region receives an average of 400 inches of snowfall annually and offers stunning views of the lake.
